<Accordion title='What all resources does NodeKeeper create?'>
    Resources that NodeKeeper creates are listed under the platform specific page. Check [here](/nodekeeper/nodekeeper-on-gcp#resources-provisioned) for GCP and [here](/nodekeeper/nodekeeper-on-aws#resources-provisioned) for AWS.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title='How much access does NodeKeeper have?'>
    The access depends on the permissions you provide to GCP serviceaccount or AWS user. NodeKeeper does not require any special permissions. It only requires permissions to create resources that are listed [here](/nodekeeper/nodekeeper-on-gcp#resources-provisioned) for GCP and [here](/nodekeeper/nodekeeper-on-aws#resources-provisioned) for AWS.
    You can also modify and implement a stricter policy for NodeKeeper.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title='How to remove NodeKeeper access?'>
    Just delete the keys from your cloud provider or delete the user/serviceaccount that you created for NodeKeeper. For SSH access, you can delete the public key from the authorized_keys file usually located in `~/.ssh/authorized_keys` directory on the node.

</Accordion>
